Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants and some other organisms use sunlight to synthesize foods from carbon dioxide and water.
The theory of relativity, developed by Albert Einstein, revolutionized the understanding of space, time, and gravity.
DNA, or deoxyribonucleic acid, is the hereditary material in humans and almost all other organisms.
The human brain is an organ that serves as the center of the nervous system and is the most complex organ in the human body.
A black hole is a region of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape from it.
Evolution is the process by which different kinds of living organisms are thought to have developed and diversified from earlier forms during the history of the earth.
The periodic table is a tabular arrangement of the chemical elements, organized on the basis of their atomic numbers, electron configurations, and recurring chemical properties.
Climate change refers to significant changes in global temperatures and weather patterns over time.
A cell is the smallest unit of life that can replicate independently, and cells are often called the "building blocks of life".
The law of conservation of energy states that the total energy of an isolated system remains constant; it is said to be conserved over time.
